How To Make Proper German Brezn (aka pretzels)

Background

The German word "Brezel" (Bavarian: Brezen) is the modern name for the baked pastry "prezzitella" (old-high-German) which was first created in the 15th century in the area of modern day Germany. The word "prezzitella" was derived from the latin word "bracchiatello", the diminuitive of "brachia" - in English: arms. The name of Brezen was derived from the word "brachia" as the pastry resembles arms crossed in front of the chest. There are so many different legends around the creation of the famous pastry that it might be interesting for you to do some research and decide which one you personally find most convincing.

Ingredients:

  • 500g flour
  • 1 pack of yeast (dry yeast)
  • 1/4 teaspoon of sugar
  • 350 ml of milk, lukewarm

  • Salt (table salt)
  • 1 1/2 litres of water
  • 2 teaspoon of baking soda
  • Salt (granulated, to sprinkle)
  • apologies for the many list-style-types but they're amazing

Instructions:

Combine flour with yeast and sugar in a bowl, add milk and salt and knead till the dough is soft and smooth. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for 45 minutes (until the volume has doubled). Roll out the dough (thin, about 20cm long) and cut it into 10-12 pieces. Roll or twist each piece, making strands, and shape them into pretzels (make sure the space between the dough is big enough as the dough will double in size and the shape would be lost).

Here is a link to a short video on:how to shape Brezen.

In a pot, boil 1.5 litres of water with 2 tablespoons of baking soda and let it boil for about 10 min. After that, add one pretzel for about 20-30 sec. (while it is inside the water, make sure it stays submerged). After 20-30 sec, take it out and let it dry.

When you’re done cooking all the pretzels, place them on a greased baking sheet (or baking paper) and sprinkle a bit salt on them (you can also add sesame, cheese, butter, etc.) and bake at 180 ° C (356 Fahrenheit) for 25-30 minutes.

Enjoy!!
